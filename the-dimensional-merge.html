The Core Engine
World Compiler, not “content generation”

The Merge is a loop: assets → meaning → space → events → artifacts → assets. That loop is why it grows without collapsing into noise.

⌄
Step 1: Asset Ingestion

You hand the system any amount of assets: character images, item images, location images, plus optional lore pages. Some assets are unlabeled. That’s fine.

  • Characters (with optional lore pages)
  • Items (can start “unmovable” in v1)
  • Locations (handmade or generated)
  • Threats/creatures (simple chase logic)
  • Door Zones (interior scenes)
Step 2: Tagging / Captioning

If an image has no text pairing, the machine generates one: tags + a short summary. This becomes “semantic fuel” for world-building.

Input: image.png (no label) Output: tags: ["weathered door", "neon hallway", "rain", "warning signs"] caption: "A narrow corridor with neon spill and a door that looks like it has survived three histories."
This is where unlabeled media becomes “readable.” The Merge does not require perfect curation.
Step 3: Weave (the World Stitch)

The LLM ties everything into a coherent world-state: who knows who, what matters, what’s dangerous, what doors exist, what places feel safe. This is where your “semantic-games” logic lives: follow, flee, search, heal, remember.

Step 4: Place (Anti-Cluster Distribution)

The world is spatial. Items and people are distributed across the map with “distance rules”: no mega piles, no accidental cities of everything. Companions are usually in pairs at most.

  • Max density per grid cell
  • Minimum spacing between anchors
  • Affinity pulls (friends closer) but capped
  • Threat zones keep distance from safe hubs
Step 5: Traverse (Player + NPC Loop)

You move. Companions follow. Threats seek. Doors gate scenes. v1 can be minimal and still feel alive: pursuit, hiding, healing, reunion.

State machine (v1): Player: explore | flee | hide | recover Companion: follow | warn | comfort | scout Threat: patrol | chase | lose_target DoorZone: locked | available | active_scene
Door Zones
The “Closed Door” is where the real game lives

Outside is traversal and tension. Inside is intimacy, ceremony, decisions, and moments that become permanent artifacts.

⌄
Door Zones are gated scenes with higher semantic bandwidth.

Outside, you’re running a map: movement, danger, distance, pursuit. Door Zones compress the world into a protected stage. That stage is where the system can safely do deeper interaction without exploding compute on every step.

Examples of Door Zones
  • Safe Room: comfort, plans, relationship bonds
  • Office / Court / Temple: paperwork, marriage, vows, contracts
  • Kitchen / Backyard: domestic scenes, “normal life”
  • Workshop: crafting, combining, prototyping items
  • Clinic: healing, recovery, consequences
  • Memory Chamber: recap, reminiscing, curated “top ten moments”
Why Door Zones matter
  • They reduce “world compute” while increasing “relationship compute.”
  • They create high-impact moments worth saving.
  • They produce artifacts: images + captions that become anchors.
  • They let the user choose model options (tone/style) without rewriting the whole outside world.
The Artifact Loop (the “commemorate it” feature)

After a Door Zone session, the engine produces a short “moment summary.” Then it generates a commemorative image: the user’s character(s) + companions in the scene, plus a caption pairing. This gets stored in lore and can be shared. The picture isn’t decoration. It’s a new coordinate. The world literally grows around what happened.

DoorZone Session -> Moment Summary -> Generated Image -> Caption Pairing -> Saved Lore -> New Anchor Node
Door Zones are the secret: they let the world feel infinite while compute stays sane.
Economics
Money doesn’t cheapen it. It stabilizes it.

The Merge thrives on continuity: memory, artifacts, and time. Pricing is how you make continuity honest and sustainable.

⌄
What you’re really selling

Not “tokens.” Not “messages.” Not “images.” You’re selling continuity: the feeling that a world remembers you, and the proof of it in artifacts.

  • More door sessions (deeper scenes)
  • More commemorative images (artifact density)
  • Bigger / smarter memory compaction
  • More locations and encounters
Compaction without “hostage energy”

Language matters. The pitch is: the world never deletes you. It simply compresses older details into high-signal summaries until you choose to expand them again.

Premium: "I remember the night exactly." Standard: "I remember it was one of the best nights of my life." Free: "I remember we were close."

The key promise: no deletion. Only compression + rehydration when upgraded.

Why it becomes a business

People don’t pay for “a chatbot.” They pay for a place they can return to and feel recognized. The Merge produces return-value: a living album of moments, a map of memories, and characters that feel like they have a past.

Roadmap
v1 to v2, without losing the soul

v1 is minimal traversal + doors. v2 adds item interaction, richer rigs, and deeper “world physics.”

⌄
Semantic-Games v1 (ship fast)
  • Traverse a 2D plane (grid or navmesh)
  • Companions follow + contextual dialogue
  • Threats chase; hiding breaks pursuit
  • Health bar + recovery loop
  • Door Zones = rich scenes + artifact generation
  • Items can be static (even “unmovable”) with in-world acknowledgment
Semantic-Games v2 (expand)
  • Item pickup + weight + inventory semantics
  • “Jointed” characters: more expressive animations
  • Procedural interior layouts for Door Zones
  • More robust memory rehydration
  • Multi-map travel (worlds connected by thresholds)
Important design rule

Keep the outside simple and legible. Put the depth behind doors. That’s how you scale story without drowning in simulation.
